Exploratorium Map Redesign Experiment

→

With the goal of increasing museum accessibility, specifically for those lacking map-reading literacy and those with sensory processing disorders, I spearheaded an experimental redesign of the Exploratorium's map.

I adapted to working in an isometric style in this experimental map, exploring if an added layer of dimension would assist visitor wayfinding. The use of color was a strong choice to make it easier to distinguish between galleries. The use of landmarks and clear communicative icons were used to help identify points of interest.

Around six drafts were created based on close examination of the existing map. An in-progress mockup was shared with co-workers around the office and on the floor to solicit feedback.
