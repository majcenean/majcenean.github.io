A piece critiquing human effects of the capitalist machine.

In exchange for the convenience of constant service, time is claimed from countless lives spent toiling across the globe in service to the consumer. As an effect of this system's prevalence in our lives, the natural world has been corrupted and a return to it is impossible.

Time begins to consume itself like an ourbourous as life is consumed by work, and the lines between day and night blur, unimportant as they confront the many faces of demand.
