Translations of the Odyssey

Lombardo, Stanley, translator. Odyssey. By Homer, Indianapolis : Hackett Publishing Company, Inc., 2000.

I have chosen to incorporate lines from Stanley Lombardo's translation of The Odyssey in my illustrative book and pedestal engravings due to the simple, brief, and plain language which he chooses to write with - without flourish, regional dialect, or long lines of prose. This style of writing is the easiest to read with the most reading comprehension in the shortest amount of time, which makes it a good choice for design work.

Powell, Barry B., translator. The Odyssey. By Homer, New York ; Oxford : Oxford University Press, 2014.

I have also read and incorporated the translation of Barry Powell to provide additional narrative context to my understanding of the epic. As this translation is written and formatted more like a storybook, it provides a fantastic and engaging prose-like quality to the poem.

Visual Bibliography: Ancient

My research was not only confined to interpretive and analytical works, but as my work involved illustration, I also looked to visual research for depictions of the characters of The Odyssey and environmental context such as ships, spears, armor, and looms. I began with ancient art to seek how residents of Classical Greece imagined these characters.
