CONCEPT

This is an interactive web game which satirizes Orientalist tendencies in physical and fictional environments.

By exploring anti-Asian racism within visual and storytelling mediums that bleeds into the real world, it calls attention to the fetishization and sexualization Asian women suffer, in addition to criticizing racist tropes and stereotypes which are commonplace in Western media.

Visual development started with the player character, Ren. I was inspired in part by Street Fighter's design for, and the fandom's sexualization of, the character Chun Li.

I developed a playful, cartoonish art style for the game as I created the first spritesheet.

I moved on to creating the non-player characters (NPCs) which would populate the world.

More NPCs, including the "yellow fever" zombies which chase the player character around the map.

Map and world development.

The beginning quadrant of the map, reminiscient of a quiet hometown. Inspired by Clement Street and Inner Richmond, Chinatown, and the Outer Sunset. Home to Ren's immediate family, who aid her on her quest.

The second quadrant of the map. More beaten down buildings. Home to The Upstart, a nameless girl forgotten by her family. Inspired by the Tenderloin.

The third quadrant of the map, a "red light district." criticizing the overt exploitation and sexualization of Asian women, both in the online world...

... and in the physical world.

The final section of the map, home to the techie king. Inspired by the Financial District. This is the location of the "final boss," a literal boss, who asks Ren to forget the murder of her parents and join forces with him.
